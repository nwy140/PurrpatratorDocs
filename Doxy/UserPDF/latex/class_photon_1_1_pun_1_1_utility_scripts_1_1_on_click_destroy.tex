\doxysubsection{Detailed Description}
Destroys the networked Game\+Object either by \doxyref{Photon\+Network.\+Destroy}{p.}{class_photon_1_1_pun_1_1_photon_network_ada7d7b9bdb306863c66b260dde7c544e} or by sending an RPC which calls Object.\+Destroy(). 

Using an RPC to Destroy a Game\+Object is typically a bad idea. It allows any player to Destroy a Game\+Object and may cause errors.

A client has to clean up the server\textquotesingle{}s event-\/cache, which contains events for Instantiate and buffered RPCs related to the GO.

A buffered RPC gets cleaned up when the sending player leaves the room, so players joining later won\textquotesingle{}t get those buffered RPCs. This in turn, may mean they don\textquotesingle{}t destroy the GO due to coming later.

Vice versa, a Game\+Object Instantiate might get cleaned up when the creating player leaves a room. This way, the Game\+Object that a RPC targets might become lost.

It makes sense to test those cases. Many are not breaking errors and you just have to be aware of them.

Gets On\+Click() calls by Unity\textquotesingle{}s IPointer\+Click\+Handler. Needs a Physics\+Raycaster on the camera. See\+: {\texttt{ https\+://docs.\+unity3d.\+com/\+Script\+Reference/\+Event\+Systems.\+IPointer\+Click\+Handler.\+html}}
